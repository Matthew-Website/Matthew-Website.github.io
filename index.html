Does advancing technology provide cures for heart disease or is it a source of a sedentary lifestyle that contributes to heart disease?

Research Topic

Many people in society today often question whether or not we place too much value on technology and modern medicine. For my topic I chose to delve further into this and really explore whether or not technology has run rampant, or for this scenario sedentary lifestyle is more of an attribute.

For the topic I plan to try and seek out research regarding the role technology plays in heart health and how far we have come in modern medicine. I of course also plan to seek out what the effects of lifestyle are on the heart, however everyone understands that lifestyle will directly affect your health and overall medical well-being.

Whilst researching this topic I will see what one can do for preventing heart disease and we can also look at what are some ways that lifestyle can help prevent it, and ways that technology can help prevent it. That would include things like supplements, annual scans, prescription medications and much more.

Articles Surrounding This Topic

When scouring the internet in preparation for discussing this topic, I came across many different articles that were explaining some parts of this topic that will assist in formulating a well-rounded answer and also possibly giving a different perspective into the matter. One research article titled, “Sedentary Behavior, Exercise, and Cardiovascular Health” mentions some of the key components to a healthy heart. They discuss the extreme heart mortality risks that come with leading a sedentary lifestyle and different heart conditions that could be prevented from living the lifestyle meant for humans to be living.

The next article I looked at was unironically from John Hopkins Medicine and the article is titled, “Sitting Disease, How A Sedentary Lifestyle Affects Heart Health”. In this article they even give analytical numbers just to show the degree of heart issues we have in America. They go over just how dangerous it is to constantly be sitting and also mention the predisposition it creates towards type-2 diabetes. In the article however they state, “sitting for long periods is directly associated with worse health outcomes including heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and cancer.”
